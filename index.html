Avoiding the brick

research

Avoiding the brick tries to frame the Android modding movement in a broader context of hacker activism, with the aim of giving a theoretical framework around this practice, that is mainly technical. Since 2009, groups of independent developers have been developing modified software for Android smartphones and distributing it for free in specialized forums. The purpose of this practice is to counterbalance the market trends such as planned obsolescence and device abandonment, used as a way to fuel trade and consumption. How has Android modding affected the smartphone market? What is the role of users/citizens in this context? What values has this movement given rise to besides technical support? How can modding live on in the contemporary smartphone market? What methodologies can be applied to other technologies?
